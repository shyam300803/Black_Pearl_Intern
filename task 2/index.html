About Porsche 911

The Porsche 911—often pronounced as “Nine Eleven” or Neunelf in German—is a legendary two-door, 2+2 high-performance sports car. Introduced by Porsche AG in September 1964, it boasts a rear-mounted flat-six engine and originally featured a torsion bar suspension.

Over the years, the 911 has undergone continuous enhancements, yet its fundamental concept remains unchanged. Notably, the engines were air-cooled until the introduction of the 996 series in 1998. The 911 has an impressive racing pedigree and has been successful in various competitions. It’s one of the most iconic sports cars globally, with unmistakable circular headlights and a distinctive silhouette that sets it apart from other models.

The Porsche 911 is a luxury sports car known for its iconic design, exceptional performance, and unparalleled driving experience.

With a history dating back to the 1960s, the 911 has evolved into one of the most coveted sports cars, combining cutting-edge technology with timeless styling.
